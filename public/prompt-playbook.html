What you can build

What can you design with AI?

AI can help design almost anything — web, mobile, or dashboards. But the quickest, highest-impact wins land in the four areas below, where it delivers a decent UI for submissions.

01

Dashboards & reporting

analytics

KPI cards, charts, filters and data tables your team scans at a glance — the headline numbers leading, detail tucked below.

02

AI chatbots

conversational

Support and assistant interfaces with every state handled — empty, typing, error — including right-to-left Arabic.

03

Workflow platforms

operations

Multi-step forms, approval chains and live status tracking that move work from request to done.

04

Admin panels

internal tools

Tables, roles and statuses for the back-office screens teams manage every day.

The basics

What is prompting?

A prompt is simply a clear, written brief. You describe what you need in plain English — who it's for, what it should do, and how it should feel — and Claude turns that brief into a working UI design. The clearer the brief, the better the result. You're not coding; you're describing intent well.

Note

This guide assumes no design or coding background. Every term is explained where it first appears — you only need to be able to describe what you want in plain English.

# The vague ask Make an HR dashboard for a government client. Show the usual HR numbers and make it look official. # What Claude is left to guess - Which government? What brand colours and fonts? - Which HR metrics matter, and in what order? - What visual references define "official"? - Charts, layout, density, Arabic / RTL support? - How should it be built and delivered?
# Brief Design a light-mode, corporate HR Dashboard for the General Secretariat (GS) Council of Ministers of Qatar. The aesthetic must match the style of the MOT ITSM Dashboard and Naufar Dashboard references — clean white card surfaces, light grey backgrounds, maroon and teal as the two primary accent colors, left sidebar navigation, and a data-rich but uncluttered layout. Government-grade. Sleek. Modern but not trendy. # Visual Style Reference (match this exactly) Overall feel: Like the MOT ITSM and Naufar dashboards — white-dominant, professional, restrained. Not dark, not flashy. Corporate confidence. — Background: #F4F6F9 or #F0F2F5 (very light cool grey — not white, not blue) — Cards: Pure white (#FFFFFF) with subtle box-shadow (0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)), 12px border-radius — Sidebar: White background, left border accent, active item highlighted in Al Adaam maroon pill — No gradients on cards. No glassmorphism. No dark surfaces. Clean and crisp. # Brand Palette — Qatar Government (GBA) Primary: — Al Adaam / Maroon: #8A1538 — primary brand accent, active states, key numbers, line charts, CTA buttons — Skyline / Navy: #0D4261 — secondary accent, AI chatbot button, section headers — Palm / Teal: #129B82 — positive trends, secondary charts, progress bars — Sea / Blue: #4194B3 — chart lines, info states Neutral/UI: Page background #F4F6F9 · Card background #FFFFFF · Border #E8ECF0 · Primary text #1A1F2E · Secondary text #6B7280 Status colors: Success #5A895A · Warning #E9C56B · Alert #DD7877 # Typography — English: "DM Sans" or "Plus Jakarta Sans" — NOT Inter — Arabic: Noto Naskh Arabic — Dashboard labels: 11px uppercase, letter-spacing 0.08em, color #6B7280 — KPI numbers: 28–36px, font-weight 700, color #1A1F2E — Card titles: 15px, font-weight 600 · Body/labels: 13px, font-weight 400 # Layout Architecture Left Sidebar (240px, white): GS logo placeholder at top · Section label "NAVIGATIONS" in 10px uppercase · Nav items: Dashboard (active — maroon pill, maroon icon + text), Reports, Schedule Reports · Section label "GLOBAL" · Nav items: User Management, Permissions Management, Audit Logs & Alerts (red badge "01"), Email Configurations · Bottom: User profile avatar + name + chevron. White sidebar with subtle right border only — no background colour changes. Top Header Bar (white, 64px, subtle bottom border): Left — page title "HR Dashboard" 18px semi-bold · Center — Date Range picker, Department dropdown, Grade dropdown, Nationality toggle (Qatari / Non-Qatari) styled as MOT "Last Month" filter badges · Right — notification bell, AR|EN language toggle, user avatar. # KPI Sections — HR Tab (12-column grid, 24px gap) All cards: white background, 12px radius, subtle shadow, 20px internal padding. Card header: title left + filter/clock icon right. Row 1 — Workforce Overview (full-width strip of 5 stat tiles) — Total Active Employees: big number + ↑ green trend badge — Qatarization Rate: percentage + thin circular arc progress in maroon — Gender Distribution: small inline horizontal bar (maroon = male, teal = female) — Vacant Positions: number in warning yellow if non-zero — Avg Tenure: number in years + sparkline Row 2 — Two equal columns Left — Attendance Rate: line chart, maroon line with circular data points, dotted grid lines, month labels on x-axis (matches MOT "Service Request Volume" style). Show 12 months. Include "Last Month" badge top right. Right — Performance Appraisal: line chart in teal (#4194B3), same card style. Show appraisal completion rate over time. Row 3 — Two equal columns Left — Training Hours: 3D-style bar chart in maroon, monthly training hours per employee. Tooltip on hover shows exact value. (Matches MOT "Change Request Volume" bar style.) Right — Leave Utilization: grouped/stacked bar chart, maroon = Leave Taken, Dune beige = Leave Remaining. Months on x-axis. (Matches MOT "SR Open And Closure Rate" dual-bar style.) Row 4 — Two equal columns Left — Recruitment Pipeline: horizontal progress bars per stage: Open Vacancies, Interviews, Offers Extended, Hires. Each bar maroon, number right, label left. (Matches MOT "SLA Breach Count" style.) Right — Turnover & Mobility: donut chart in maroon/salmon/teal for Active Employees, Turnover, Transfers. Center total number. (Matches MOT bottom-right card style.) # AI Chatbot Button Bottom right corner: pill-shaped button, navy (#0D4261) background, white text "AI Assistant", maroon/teal animated avatar icon left, subtle glow pulse on icon. On click: side panel slides in from right (400px, white). Header "GS Assistant / مساعد الديوان" + close. Suggested chips: "Qatarization Rate?", "My Leave Balance", "HR Policy". User bubbles: right-aligned, maroon bg, white text. Bot bubbles: left-aligned, #F4F6F9, dark text. Typing indicator: 3 dots in teal. # Animations (subtle, professional — not flashy) — Cards fade up staggered on load (opacity 0→1, translateY 12px→0, 60ms delay between cards) — KPI numbers count up from 0 on load (600ms ease-out) — Chart lines draw in left to right on load · Bar charts grow up from baseline — Hover on cards: subtle shadow lift only (no scale transform — too playful for gov) — Active sidebar item: smooth background transition · Chatbot panel: ease-out slide from right # Key Aesthetic Rules ✓ Match MOT and Naufar dashboard screenshots exactly in density and card style ✓ White cards, light grey background — no dark mode, no glassmorphism ✓ Maroon (#8A1538) is the primary accent everywhere — charts, active states, buttons ✓ Teal (#129B82) as secondary chart color only · Navy (#0D4261) for AI button and section accents only ✓ All text readable at small sizes — government data dashboards are information-dense ✓ No decorative illustrations, no consumer-app icons ✓ RTL-ready layout structure (even if English-only in the mockup) # Deliverable A single, self-contained HTML file. All CSS and JavaScript inline. ECharts for all charts. No external npm dependencies. No build step. Opens directly in a browser and deploys to Netlify by drag-and-drop. The result must feel like it belongs in the same product family as the MOT ITSM dashboard — corporate, trusted, Qatar government grade.

# The vague ask Build an executive dashboard for QFZ's CEO. Show investments, HR, operations and finance, and make it look premium. # What Claude is left to guess - Which visual benchmark defines "premium" and "executive"? - QFZ's exact brand colours, fonts and logo usage? - Which KPIs lead, and how each screen is structured? - Which chart types to use — and which to avoid? - Motion language, spacing system, information density?
# Brief Design a premium, light-mode Executive Intelligence Platform for the CEO of Qatar Free Zones Authority (QFZ). This is not an admin dashboard — it is a strategic command center used by the CEO and executive leadership to monitor investments, investors, free-zone operations, workforce, development progress and organisational KPIs. # Visual benchmark (match this exactly) Emulate a fusion of Bloomberg Executive Analytics, Palantir Foundry, a Deloitte CEO Cockpit and a McKinsey Digital Command Center — anchored to the supplied QFZ Observatory reference as the primary visual benchmark. Premium enterprise feel · white-dominant · light-grey canvas · large visual storytelling · executive KPI hierarchy · modern data viz · high-end motion · minimal noise. It must feel expensive, trustworthy and government-grade. Avoid: generic admin templates, Bootstrap look, SaaS-startup aesthetics, glassmorphism, neon, gaming UI, dark mode. # Brand system — QFZ Primary — QFZ Blue #2540A3 · QFZ Burgundy #862041 Supporting — White #FFFFFF · Background #F5F7FA · Dark text #1A1F2E · Secondary #6B7280 · Borders #E8ECF0 Status — Success #129B82 · Warning #E9C56B · Alert #DD7877 # Typography Primary font Helvetica Neue (fallback Inter). Page titles 36px/700 · Section headers 16px/600 · Card titles 15px/600 · Labels 12px/500 · KPI values 36–48px/700 · Small metrics 24px/600. Institutional and executive throughout. # Global layout Left sidebar (260px, white, subtle right border): QFZ logo + "Executive Intelligence Platform" with a "CEO Command Center" subtitle. Nav — Overview, Investments & Development, Development & Operations, Human Capital, Finance, Strategy, Reports, Settings. Active item = QFZ Blue pill, white text, soft shadow. Bottom — user profile, language toggle, system status, last sync time. Charts draw left-to-right, staggered 600–1200ms. Page load: cards fade-up, translateY 20px, opacity 0→1, 80ms stagger. Hover: subtle shadow increase only, no scale. # Landing — executive home Hero "Good Morning, CEO" panel — date, weather, last data refresh, system health, over an animated QFZ geometric brand pattern. Executive snapshot strip of five KPI cards (Total Investors, Investment Value, Licenses Issued, Active Projects, Employees), each with a large number, mini trend chart, trend % and animated counter. Application launcher (Employee Services, CRM, ERP, Approvals, Dashboards) — large cards, elevated with a soft blue glow on hover. KPI performance: L3/L4 2026 and 2026-7 results vs targets, leads generated, licenses issued — premium KPI cards with sparklines, trend badges, animated counters. CAPEX performance: large dual-line chart (target / actual / forecast). Investor Application Tracker: a massive interactive donut (Submitted → Approved → License Granted → Construction Started → Completed); clicking a segment opens a slide-out detail panel (investor count, investment value, conversion %, pipeline). Deal Pipeline Health: a horizontal connected pipeline, each deal an interactive node coloured by health, with a right-side intelligence panel (upcoming approvals, risks, delayed projects, opportunities). Investor Portfolio: interactive world map of investor origins + sector treemap + bubble distribution. Sector Analysis: animated bubble chart (growth, investment volume, opportunity, market concentration). # Development & Operations Hero component: a full-width interactive GIS-style free-zone map — hover highlights a parcel, click opens a slide-out (investor, area, sector, utilities, status, allocation). Land analytics: allocated / available area, utilization %, pending allocation — with heatmap, allocation trends and sector mix. # Human Capital Workforce KPI strip (headcount, Qatarization, gender mix, leadership ratio, attrition) · interactive org structure with department drill-down · attendance trend + heatmap calendar + presence score · recruitment funnel (applications → interviews → offers → hires) · L&D (training hours, certifications, completion rate, skills) · performance distribution · travel & expense. # AI assistant Fixed bottom-right — "Microsoft Copilot meets Bloomberg Assistant": QFZ Blue, white text, subtle pulse. Click opens a 420px right-side panel. Suggested prompts: "Show delayed investors", "Why did investment growth drop?", "Summarize workforce performance", "Show CAPEX variance". # Data-visualization rules Prefer treemap, Sankey, bubble, radial, heatmap, animated area charts, interactive maps and progress rings. Avoid basic pie charts, Excel-looking visuals, dense tables, 3D charts and gauge charts. # Deliverable A single, self-contained HTML file — all CSS and JavaScript inline, real interactive and animated charts, no build step. CEO-first hierarchy, white cards on a light-grey canvas, everything animated, enterprise-grade polish — a client-ready executive experience suitable for direct presentation to QFZ leadership.

Common mistakes

Poor prompt vs. better prompt

The same request, two ways. Notice how little extra effort the better version takes.

Poor prompt
Vague — Claude has to guess
"Make me a nice dashboard."
No audience — who is it for?
No goal — what should it achieve?
"Nice" means nothing measurable.
No references, no role, no constraints.
Better prompt
Specific — Claude has what it needs
"You are a senior dashboard designer. [2 refs + brand] A sales dashboard for regional managers: this month's revenue first, a trend chart, top 5 deals. Confident and clean, like Linear. ECharts, Lucide icons, WCAG AA."
Sets a role and attaches references.
Sets the data hierarchy: revenue, then trend, then deals.
Gives a quality bar: "clean, like Linear."
Names the UI basics: real charts, real icons, AA.
